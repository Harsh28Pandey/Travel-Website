The Eiffel Tower

A Symbol of Paris

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower. Constructed in 1889 as the entrance to the 1889 World's Fair, it has become both a global icon of France and one of the most recognizable structures in the world. The tower is 324 metres (1,063 feet) tall, about the same height as an 81-story building. It is made up of 18,000 pieces of wrought iron, which were riveted together.
